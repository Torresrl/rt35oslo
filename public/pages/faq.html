What are the alcohol laws in Norway?

The alcohol laws in Norway are quite strict. Beer and beverages with up to 4.7% can be bought in most supermarkets until 20:00 on weekdays and 18:00 on Saturdays. Wine, spirits or other beverages with more than 4.7% can only be bought in shops called “Vinmonopolet” until 18:00 on weekdays and 15:00 on Saturdays. It’s not allowed to drink in public outside of a serving area for a bar or restaurant. You can be fined by the police if they see it. It’s not allowed to bring along or enjoy alcohol (or any other beverages for that matter) in bars or restaurants. Bars and nightclubs can’t serve alcohol before 10:00 and have to close 03:00 the latest. The legal drinking age for beer, wine and cider is 18. 20 for spirits such as vodka, gin and rum.

What is the currency in Norway?

The currency in Norway is Norwegian Krone.

What is the most common form of payment in Norway?

It’s mostly common to use credit/debit cards. The most common types are BankAxept, VISA, or Mastercards. AMEX is getting more and more common but is not always accepted. Cash is not widely accepted. Most hotels, shops, bars, and nightclubs won’t accept it.

How expensive is eating and drinking out in Norway?

It can get a bit expensive eating and drinking out. A beer can be around 11 €, or a coffee can be 6 €. However, we’ve made some good deals at some of the places we’re visiting.

Is tipping expected in Norway?

Tipping is not expected in Norway, as a service charge is usually included in the bill.

What should I keep in mind when interacting with Norwegians?

It's important to respect personal space and privacy, as Norwegians tend to be reserved and private.
